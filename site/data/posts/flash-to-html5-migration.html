Flash development technology is becoming less and less relevant as time goes on. On one hand, Adobe is focusing its efforts on the graphics side and less on code; on the other, Apple and Google have announced that they do not support Adobe's player. The result is that many applications that relied on Flash are losing their relevance for the large portion of the population using Chrome or Safari browsers.

Many organizations that hold Flash applications are starting to look for alternatives, yet they want to preserve as much as possible of the original investment made in Flash development.

OneCode offers a perfect solution that combines using the core code written in Flash and migrating it to the modern world of HTML5. This way there is no need to redevelop the application from scratch, while still being able to enjoy the capabilities of HTML5 and the promise of long-term support for years to come.

Who Is a Migration from a Legacy Flash Application to HTML5 Right For?

  1. Applications without complex graphics
  2. Applications required to run across various browsers and on mobile
  3. Medium-sized ActionScript 3.0 applications — those with significant development investment, where rebuilding from scratch would be relatively costly
  4. Small ActionScript 2.0 applications — the source can be leveraged to create a faster foundation for a new application

Is It Time to Make the Switch?

Despite HTML5 being a hot topic and Flash having become almost a dirty word, not everyone needs to rush to make the switch. There are Flash applications that will continue to serve their purposes well for several more years. But if an application is no longer meeting its commercial goals, that is definitely the time to consider an upgrade or replacement.

Is HTML5 Mature Enough?

HTML5 is in a phase of rapid development, and today it enables a broad range of capabilities. The language still does not deliver strong performance in the realm of rich graphics compared to alternatives like Unity or the Unreal Engine, but in every other domain its advantages are clear — especially when looking at its long-term benefits.

How Does the Migration Process from Flash to HTML5 Work?

After reviewing the existing assets in the application, we evaluate whether it makes sense to pursue a migration path, to develop a new application from scratch, or even to stay with the existing application. We then select the appropriate tools that allow for an easy, fast, and cost-effective transition while also enabling ongoing future development and preserving the project's value in light of market changes. In the final phase, the OneCode team extracts the core codebase and adapts it to HTML5. The result will always be an effective application that meets the defined objectives.
